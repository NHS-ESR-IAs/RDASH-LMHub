What are Learning Half Days?

RDaSH wants to help all staff learn and grow so every month the Trust gives everyone a protected half day just for learning.

Learning isn’t just online courses; you can build your skills in lots of ways.

Learning half days give you time to learn, think, and get better at your job.

How can you use your learning half day?

Learning isn't just online courses; you can build your skills in lots of ways. Learning Half Days give you time to learn, think, and get better at your job.

You can use it to:

  • Work together with your team
  • Learn new ways to help patients, colleagues, and families, including your own
  • Work on getting better and learning more for yourself
  • Try something new or share what you know
  • Do your important mandatory and statutory training

In this Learning Hub, you'll find lots of useful resources, classes, and chances to meet others who also want to learn. Everything is designed to help you share ideas and do well. Jump in, explore, and let's learn together!

Self Service Training Portal

Your Gateway to Training: Self-enroll for your own development or manage your team's training from one central dashboard. Please note: this guide is for non-MAST courses.

Employee Self-Enrolment Guide

01

Access My Learning

On your ESR dashboard, click the My Learning link in the sidebar to open your learner homepage.

02

Search for Training

Use the search bar on your learner page. Type in a keyword from the course name and click the magnifying glass icon.

03

Select and Enrol

Select your course to see available classes. Click the Enrol Cog for the specific class you want. Review the details, add any special instructions, and click Submit.

04

Check Enrollment

The course will now appear in your Instructor-Led Enrolments section on your learning home page.

How to Cancel

Click the Class Name on your learning page. Use the drop-down to select Unenrol, click Go, select your reason, and click Finish.

Manager & Admin Enrolment Guide

01

Access Team Learning

Click the Manager tab, select My Team Career Information, and then click Learning.

02

Identify Staff Member

Search for the staff member by name or select them from the list. Click the Action button (circled in yellow) to proceed to their learner page.

03

Search & Enrol

Type the course keyword into the Search Course bar. Select the correct class by clicking the Enrol Cog.

04

Confirmation

Add any special instructions and click Submit. The course will now show in the staff member's Instructor-Led Enrolments section.

How to Cancel

Click the Class Name, select Unenrol from the drop-down, and click Go. Choose a reason and click Finish.

Technical & Account Support

I’m having technical problems. Who do I contact?

Contact the IT helpdesk using their online portal.

I forgot my ESR login, or ESR will not load. What do I do?

Contact the IT helpdesk using their portal.

Do I need to be logged in to ESR to join a course?

Yes. When you try to book a course, the system will ask you to log in.

Can I use the learning hub on my mobile?

Yes. You can use it if you have the link.

Finding & Managing Your Training

Where do I find my mandatory training (MAST)?

Search for MAST in the Course Prospectus (Learner Catalogue panel).

How do I find courses and join them?

Go to the Learner panel for help and the user guide.

Where can I see the courses I’m booked on, past courses, and my certificates?

You can see all of these in ESR, just like before.

How do I book myself onto face-to-face (non-MAST) training?

Go to the Learner panel for help and the user guide.

Can I watch a training session after it has finished?

If it was recorded, it will be in the Video Vault panel once uploaded.

Has my ESR changed because of the new learning hub?

No. Nothing has changed. The hub just helps you find the right ESR pages more quickly.
